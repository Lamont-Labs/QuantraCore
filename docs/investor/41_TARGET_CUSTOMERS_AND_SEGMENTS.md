## Institutional Segments

### Segment 1: Small/Mid Quantitative Funds

**Profile:**
- AUM: $50M - $500M
- Team: 5-20 people
- Strategy: Systematic, often single-strategy
- Tech: Building or buying infrastructure

**Why They Need This:**
- Can't afford to build from scratch
- Need deterministic, auditable signals
- Want research infrastructure, not execution
- Value provenance for investor reporting

**Challenges:**
- Price-sensitive
- May have existing tools
- Integration complexity
- Skeptical of external signals

**Fit Score:** High

---

### Segment 2: Prop Trading Shops

**Profile:**
- Capital: Firm capital, not client assets
- Team: Traders with varying tech skills
- Strategy: Discretionary or systematic
- Regulation: Lighter than fund managers

**Why They Need This:**
- Research edge without ML team
- Structural signals to complement intuition
- Paper trail for internal review
- Risk controls for trader oversight

**Challenges:**
- Often prefer internal tools
- "Not invented here" syndrome
- Fast-moving, impatient buyers
- Want immediate P&L impact

**Fit Score:** Medium-High

---

### Segment 3: Broker/Platforms

**Profile:**
- Business: Execution and custody services
- Clients: Retail or institutional traders
- Differentiation: Value-added services
- Tech: Platform-based

**Why They Need This:**
- Differentiate with intelligence layer
- Increase client engagement
- White-label opportunity
- Reduce client churn

**Challenges:**
- Integration with existing platforms
- Regulatory complexity
- Liability concerns
- Long sales cycles

**Fit Score:** Medium

---

### Segment 4: Family Offices

**Profile:**
- AUM: Variable, often $100M+
- Team: Small, often outsourced
- Strategy: Multi-asset, long-term
- Decision: Principal-driven

**Why They Need This:**
- Sophisticated analysis without team
- Independence from external advisors
- Research for allocation decisions
- Due diligence on managers

**Challenges:**
- Conservative tech adoption
- Long decision cycles
- May prefer advice over tools
- Relationship-driven sales

**Fit Score:** Medium

---

## Retail/Prosumer Segments

### Segment 5: Active Retail Traders

**Profile:**
- Capital: $10K - $500K
- Activity: Daily/weekly trading
- Tools: Multiple platforms and apps
- Education: Self-taught or courses

**Why They Need This (QuantraVision):**
- Institutional-grade analysis accessible
- Visual overlays on mobile
- Educational framing
- Pattern recognition assistance

**Challenges:**
- Price-sensitive
- High churn
- Support burden
- Expectation management

**Fit Score:** Medium-High (for QuantraVision)

---

### Segment 6: Technical Analysis Enthusiasts

**Profile:**
- Activity: Chart reading as hobby/profession
- Content: Create or consume TA content
- Community: Active in trading communities
- Tools: Multiple charting platforms

**Why They Need This:**
- Quantified structural analysis
- Validation of manual patterns
- Content creation material
- Educational tool

**Challenges:**
- May prefer manual analysis
- Skeptical of automation
- Price-sensitive
- Fragmented community

**Fit Score:** Medium

## Customer Personas

### Persona 1: "Quant Fund CTO"

**Name:** Alex, CTO at $200M systematic fund
**Pain:** Building research infrastructure from scratch
**Goal:** Auditable signals for investment committee
**Budget:** $50K-$200K/year for tools
**Decision:** Leads tech decisions, influences investment

**Message:** "Deterministic research infrastructure that your LPs can understand."

### Persona 2: "Prop Trader Manager"

**Name:** Sam, Head Trader at prop shop
**Pain:** Traders using inconsistent methods
**Goal:** Standardized analysis for all traders
**Budget:** $20K-$100K/year
**Decision:** Controls tool budget

**Message:** "Structural signals that complement trader intuition with audit trail."

### Persona 3: "Retail Day Trader"

**Name:** Jordan, active retail trader
**Pain:** Too much information, no structure
**Goal:** Quick structural read on any chart
**Budget:** $10-$50/month
**Decision:** Personal, fast

**Message:** "Institutional analysis in your pocket, no signals, just structure."
